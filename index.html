About

I work on the intersection of wireless communications, machine learning, and high-performance computing. I am particularly interested in building practical AI-driven control mechanisms for radio access networks, including RL-based schedulers, simulation platforms, and GPU-accelerated implementations.

Research Interests

AI for Wireless Networks

Reinforcement learning and data-driven optimization for scheduling, resource allocation, and network control in RAN systems.

GPU-Accelerated Communication Systems

CUDA-based acceleration and high-performance implementation for communication signal processing and RAN software systems.

Simulation and System Prototyping

End-to-end experimentation using Python, C/C++, and MATLAB for communication-system simulation and algorithm development.

Future Directions

Scalable AI systems for wireless infrastructure, robust online adaptation, and practical deployment of learning-based control.

Selected Projects

RL-based RAN Scheduler

Designing reinforcement learning approaches for wireless resource scheduling, with emphasis on throughput, fairness, and latency-aware control.

cuPHY Test Development

Implementing and organizing test code for example components in CUDA-accelerated RAN software, focusing on maintainability and verification.

MATLAB-based Wireless Simulation

Building simulation environments for communication-system evaluation and RL integration, including step-wise execution and policy experimentation.

AI-RAN Research Exploration

Investigating practical directions for combining machine learning with radio access networks, from policy control to system-level implementation.
